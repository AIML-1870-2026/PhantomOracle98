What do Torino & Palermo scales mean?

Torino Scale (0–10): Public communication tool. 0 = no hazard; 1–3 = merit monitoring; 4–7 = threatening; 8–10 = certain impact. Nearly all tracked objects are 0.

Palermo Scale: Technical hazard comparison relative to background risk. Negative values = less risky than chance; ≥ 0 = warrants attention; ≥ 1 = exceptional. Most objects score around −3 to −6.
